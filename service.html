Core Functions of VETRA (Vehicle Traffic Regulation Authority)

1. Traffic Management and Control

VETRA ensures the smooth flow of traffic across Kano’s road network, especially on major routes like Zaria Road and Gwarzo Road. This involves the enforcement of traffic signals, promotion of lane discipline, and deployment of traffic personnel at key junctions.

2. Enforcement of Traffic Regulations

The agency penalizes violations such as:

  • Disobeying traffic lights
  • Illegal parking
  • Vehicle overloading (e.g., tricycles and buses)
  • Using phones or eating while driving

VETRA works in close collaboration with the Nigeria Police Force and the Federal Road Safety Corps (FRSC) to uphold law and order on the roads.

3. Vehicle Registration and Licensing

Responsible for the issuance and renewal of:

  • Vehicle number plates
  • Driver’s licenses

This ensures that all vehicles on the road comply with safety and roadworthiness standards.

4. Vehicle Inspections

Regular inspections are conducted to verify compliance with safety and regulatory standards—especially for commercial vehicles like Keke Napep (tricycles), buses, and trucks.

5. Driver Education and Training

VETRA offers educational programs and training to promote:

  • Awareness of traffic laws
  • Safe driving habits
  • Reduction of road accidents

6. Regulation of Commercial Vehicles

The agency monitors and regulates operations of:

  • Tricycles (Keke Napep)
  • Motorcycles (Okada)
  • Commercial buses

These vehicles must meet all licensing and operational standards. For example, tricycle license permit fees were recently increased from ₦8,000 to ₦100,000 as part of a regulatory overhaul.

7. Public Awareness Campaigns

VETRA actively engages the public through:

  • Road safety sensitization campaigns
  • Community outreach programs
  • Stakeholder consultations to improve transportation policy and compliance
